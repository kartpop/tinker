### Wave-Particle Duality

Wave-particle duality is the concept that every particle or quantum entity can be described as either a particle or a wave, depending on the experimental context. This was one of the key ideas that emerged from early quantum experiments, and it highlights the strange and fascinating nature of quantum entities.

#### Key Points:

1. **Dual Nature**: Electrons, photons (light particles), and other quantum entities can exhibit properties of both particles (with mass and position) and waves (with frequency and wavelength). For example, light can behave as electromagnetic waves, but it can also be observed as particles of light called photons.

2. **Double-Slit Experiment**: One of the most famous experiments demonstrating wave-particle duality is the double-slit experiment. When light or electrons are sent through two closely spaced slits, they create an interference pattern on a screen, indicating wave-like behavior. However, if individual particles are sent one by one, they still create the interference pattern, suggesting that each particle behaves like a wave. When trying to measure which slit the particle goes through, the interference pattern disappears, showing particle-like behavior.

3. **Implications**: This duality implies that quantum entities do not fit neatly into classical concepts of particles or waves. Instead, they exist in a probability state described by a wave function until measured, which collapses the wave function into a definite state.

### Why It Matters

Understanding wave-particle duality helps in grasping how at a fundamental level, the universe doesn’t always conform to our everyday experiences. It opens the door to further topics in quantum mechanics, such as superposition and entanglement.
